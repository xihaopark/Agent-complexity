1. **Registry workflow_id vs literature_map workflow_id mismatch** — one workflow uses `snakemake-workflows-msisensor-pro-finish` in registry but `microsatellite-instability-detection-with-msisensor-pro-finish` in the literature map. Rather than rename either, I added the former as an alias in `experiments/skills/manifest.json::by_workflow_id` pointing to the same DOI. Documented in the manifest `notes`.

2. **systemPipeR DOI shared by 5 workflows** — I generated `experiments/skills/10.1186_s12859-016-0938-4/SKILL.md` once and point all 5 tgirke workflow_ids at it (only 1 paper-skill billing event). None of these 5 workflows have tasks in `registry.real.json`, so this is only for E3 completeness, not 32-task coverage.

3. **Paperless tasks** — 14 of the 32 tasks have no paper coverage. Per the playbook ("Paper is allowed to be missing for the 2 workflows C3 couldn't map"), more than 2 are missing here because several of the registry's workflows (chipseq, RiyaDua, rna-longseq, joncahn, spilterlize) were never in the literature map to begin with. C3's `workflow_literature_map.json` only covers the 30 original `finish` workflows; the newer registry adds two workflows (chipseq, rna-longseq) that aren't in the literature map and several more (RiyaDua, joncahn) whose DOI search returned nothing usable. Paper-arm smoke confirms graceful fallback, so E3 can proceed.

4. **Manifest size / inline truncation** — `skill_md_inline` is capped at 4000 chars (same cap A2/B2 used), so batch_runner's prompt splicing does not balloon.
